## PART 4: BEST PRACTICES FOR ACADEMIC PORTFOLIO WEBSITES

### What Award-Winning Academic Sites Get Right

Based on research from the **2025 Best Personal Academic Websites Contest** (The Academic Designer) and multiple best-practice guides:

**1. Clear positioning from the first screen**
- The best academic sites lead with a value statement: who you are, what you research, and why it matters
- Example from a contest winner: "I believe in making them accessible and impactful through science communication, data visualization, and engaging digital content"
- Christopher's equivalent might be: "I pioneer work at the intersection of new writing and emerging technologies, exploring how AI is reshaping human creativity"

**2. Standard academic portfolio pages**
According to Rice University, Berkeley, UC Press, and The Academic Designer, most academic websites include:
- **Home/About** -- brief bio, photo, contact, positioning statement
- **Research** -- interests, publications, current projects
- **CV** -- downloadable PDF, ideally also displayed as a page
- **Teaching** -- courses, philosophy, student outcomes
- **Media/Press** -- coverage, interviews, talks
- **Contact** -- professional email, social links

**3. Design principles for academics**
From Berkeley's Townsend Center:
- Be consistent with formatting, fonts, colours
- No more than 2 different fonts
- White or neutral background, black or charcoal text, 1-2 accent colours
- Design like a billboard, not a newspaper -- negative space is your friend
- Keep navigation simple and visible on all pages

**4. Content principles**
- Write no more than a few sentences per item; use links for depth
- Include bullet points and links rather than long prose blocks
- Every project should have: what it is, who funded it, what the impact was, and a link or embed
- A downloadable CV is essential

**5. What differentiates creative academic sites**
Christopher's case is unusual because he is both a creative practitioner AND an academic researcher. The best sites in this hybrid space:
- Separate the academic portfolio (publications, grants, teaching) from the creative portfolio (projects, performances, podcasts)
- But link them together through a coherent narrative
- Use the creative work as evidence of research impact
- Embed media (podcast players, video) rather than just describing projects

### Technical Best Practices for Static Portfolio Sites

For a Claude Code-built site specifically:

**1. Framework options**
- **Hugo** -- very fast static site generator; the academic community has several purpose-built themes (Pascal Michaillat's minimalist Hugo template is specifically designed for academic websites). Free hosting on GitHub Pages.
- **Next.js/Astro** -- more flexibility for interactive elements like podcast embeds, but more complex
- **Plain HTML/CSS** -- simplest to maintain, but less scalable
- **11ty (Eleventy)** -- good middle ground; Markdown-based content, fast, flexible

**2. Hosting**
- GitHub Pages (free), Netlify (free tier), Vercel (free tier), Cloudflare Pages (free)
- All support custom domains
- Eliminates the monthly Squarespace subscription

**3. Content management**
- Markdown files for all content -- easily editable by Claude Code or by hand
- Front matter (YAML) for metadata (title, date, category, tags)
- Separation of content from presentation
- Git-based version control

**4. SEO essentials**
- Proper meta titles and descriptions per page
- Structured data / schema markup for academic content
- Open Graph tags for social sharing
- Sitemap.xml and robots.txt
- Fast page load times (static sites excel here)
