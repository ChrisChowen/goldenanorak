About

Christopher Hogg

I explore how technology reshapes the stories we tell, the ways we learn, and the people we become.

My work sits at the intersection of new writing, emerging technologies, and the human experience. I make podcasts, plays, operas, memoir, and research-led experiments in AI and education. I build things that use narrative as a way of understanding.

Download CV Press Photo

Christopher Hogg is a creative technologist, educator, and award-winning playwright exploring how AI changes the way we create, learn, and tell stories.

He is a Lecturer in Creative Digital & Social Media in the Department of Media Arts at Royal Holloway, University of London, and is connected to the StoryFutures / CoSTAR National Lab for Creative Technologies. He is based in Brighton.

With 8 years of teaching at Royal Holloway and 17+ years prior experience in the digital marketing industry, Christopher’s work sits at the intersection of new writing and emerging technologies. His creative projects, from BBC award-winning podcast drama to a 425-page memoir exploring AI and trauma, pioneer new forms of storytelling for digital audiences.

His most recent public work explores how AI is reshaping human creativity, how students perceive their own intelligence relative to AI, and how assessment in higher education must evolve. His articles in Times Higher Education and Wonkhe have generated significant discussion across the sector.

He also writes about men in trouble, because he is one.
