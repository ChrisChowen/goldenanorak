Christopher Hogg is a writer, educator, and creative technologist whose work moves between audio drama, performance, memoir, and public writing about AI and creativity. He holds an MA from Goldsmiths, University of London and is currently undertaking PhD research. Before entering academia, he spent 17+ years working in the creative industries.

He is Lecturer in Creative Digital & Social Media at Royal Holloway, University of London, where his teaching and research focus on how digital technologies reshape creative practice, storytelling, and education. He is connected to StoryFutures and the CoSTAR National Lab for Creative Technologies. His research interests include AI and creativity, digital storytelling, practice-based research, and the pedagogy of creative technology.

His creative practice includes Rathband: A Digital Tragedy (BBC Podcast of the Year, iTunes #1, 30,000+ downloads, featuring Douglas Rushkoff, Arts Council England funded), Cassie & Corey (a podcast musical for young audiences), Il Letto (an opera about Puccini’s deathbed), and 9 Holidays I Wish I Had Never Been On (a serialised memoir on Substack exploring trauma, AI, and synthetic memory).

His public writing includes articles in Times Higher Education and Wonkhe. He has lectured internationally, including a university tour across India (Bangalore & Mumbai, January 2026), and presented at the Cambridge Conference on AI & Creativity (October 2024). He is a TEDxUAL speaker.

He is currently developing a MOOC on Narrative Foundations for Immersive Media and Video Games (2026–2029) at Royal Holloway. His work sits at the intersection of creative practice, practice-based research, and public argument about what happens when technology enters the classroom.

Based in Brighton.
